How dice cricket works

Every ball is a roll of two dice. The pair decides the outcome — runs, a dot, a boundary, or pressure on the batsman.

Doubles are strikes. Matching dice put a strike on the striker; fill the strike meter (6 strikes) and he’s out. Player ratings and live averages tilt the odds.

Boundaries: a 4–5 pair is FOUR; 1–6 is TWO SIXES — 12 runs off one ball.

Phases matter. Powerplay boosts boundaries, the middle overs favour bowlers, and the death is pure chaos.

Seeds are random — every simulation is a fresh match.
